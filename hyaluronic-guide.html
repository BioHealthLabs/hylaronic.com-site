What is hyaluronic acid?

Hyaluronic acid is a molecule naturally found in skin, eyes and joints. It can bind large amounts of water, which helps keep skin looking cushioned and comfortable.

In skincare, “hyaluronic acid” often refers to several related forms used in serums and creams to increase surface hydration and improve skin feel.

What does hyaluronic acid actually do?

  • Boosts surface hydration: helps skin look smoother and more dewy.
  • Softens fine lines (temporarily): plumps the top layers of the skin.
  • Improves product feel: creates that “bouncy gel” texture in serums.
  • Supports barrier comfort: hydrated skin usually tolerates actives better.
Reality check: HA hydrates. It does not replace sunscreen, retinoids, or professional treatments for deep wrinkles.

Who should use hyaluronic acid?

  • Dry or dehydrated skin: extra hydration under moisturiser.
  • Oily skin: lightweight hydration without heavy creams.
  • Retinoid / acid users: offsets dryness from strong actives.
  • Dry indoor air: heating/AC can increase water loss.

How to use hyaluronic acid correctly

  1. Cleanse with a gentle, non-stripping cleanser.
  2. On slightly damp skin, apply a thin layer of HA serum (a few drops).
  3. Follow with a moisturiser to seal in the hydration.
  4. Use SPF 30+ every morning as usual.

If you apply HA on bone-dry skin and skip moisturiser, it can feel tight — especially in low-humidity air.

Common forms of hyaluronic acid

  • Sodium hyaluronate: common salt form used in many serums.
  • Hydrolyzed hyaluronic acid: smaller fragments designed to sit/behave differently on skin.
  • Sodium acetylated hyaluronate: modified form often used for “cling” and comfort.

Many modern serums combine multiple weights. If you want the “deep dive”, read the advanced HA guide.

How often can you use hyaluronic acid?

Most people can use HA once or twice daily. If something feels sticky, pills under makeup, or breaks you out, it’s usually the overall formula — not HA itself.

Biggest mistakes people make

  • Using it without moisturiser: HA needs an occlusive layer on top.
  • Applying too much: more product usually equals more tackiness.
  • Stacking multiple HA products: redundant and can feel gross.
  • Expecting wrinkle “repair”: hydration is not structural remodeling.

Can you combine HA with other actives?

  • Vitamin C: HA after vitamin C can reduce dryness.
  • Retinoids: HA supports comfort in otherwise drying routines.
  • Niacinamide: barrier-friendly combination for many people.

How to choose a good HA serum

  • Prefer fragrance-free formulas if you’re sensitive.
  • Look for supporting humectants (glycerin, panthenol, urea).
  • Avoid heavy, sticky gels if you’re oily/acne-prone.
  • Don’t overpay for marketing — mid-priced formulas can perform extremely well.

This guide is for general information only and is not medical advice. If you have persistent skin concerns, talk to your own clinician or dermatologist.

Hyaluronic Acid FAQ

Can hyaluronic acid make my skin drier?

It can feel that way if you apply it to completely dry skin and skip moisturiser. Apply on damp skin and seal it in.

Can I use hyaluronic acid every day?

Yes — most people can use it daily (often twice daily). If you react, it’s typically the overall formula.

Do I still need moisturiser if I use HA?

Yes. HA binds water; moisturiser helps keep it there.
