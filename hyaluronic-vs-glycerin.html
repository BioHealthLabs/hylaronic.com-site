Hyaluronic Acid vs Glycerin

Hyaluronic acid and glycerin are both humectants – ingredients that attract water into the skin. They’re often talked about as competitors, but in reality they work best together.

How they work in the skin

  • Hyaluronic acid can hold many times its weight in water and sits mainly on the surface.
  • Glycerin is a smaller molecule that can draw water into the upper layers of the skin.

In simple terms: hyaluronic acid feels more “plumping” on the surface, while glycerin gives deep, flexible hydration.

Which one is more hydrating?

On paper, hyaluronic acid sounds stronger because of its famous “1000x its weight in water” claim. In real routines, glycerin is just as important – and often more reliable in very dry climates.

That’s why many of the best serums and moisturisers use both ingredients together instead of choosing just one.

Which one is better for sensitive skin?

Both are generally well-tolerated, even by sensitive skin. Problems usually come from the overall formula: fragrance, alcohol or too many actives layered on top.

If your skin is reactive, look for simple formulas with hyaluronic acid and glycerin plus soothing ingredients like panthenol or allantoin – and minimal extras.

When to focus on one over the other

  • Very tight, dehydrated skin: look for serums and creams combining hyaluronic acid + glycerin.
  • Oily but dehydrated: lightweight glycerin-based products can feel less sticky.
  • Layer-heavy routines: a single good serum containing both is often better than stacking many products.

If you’re still unsure, think of it this way: hyaluronic acid gives you that immediate, plump feel on the surface. Glycerin quietly keeps the hydration going in the background – and the best formulas use both.
