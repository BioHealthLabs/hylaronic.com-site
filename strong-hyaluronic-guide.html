Why “HA makes me dry” happens

Hyaluronic acid binds water. But it does not create an occlusive seal. In dry air, water can evaporate quickly unless you add a moisturiser on top. Some formulas also rely on film-formers that can feel tight when they dry.

  • Dry environment: heated indoor air + low humidity increases evaporation.
  • Wrong application: applied to dry skin and not sealed with cream.
  • Too much product: thick gel layers dry down sticky or tight.
  • Formula design: heavy thickeners/film-formers can create “tight mask” feeling.

Molecular weight: what it means (practically)

Brands talk about “low” and “high” molecular weight HA. The important part is how the product feels and layers:

  • Higher weight HA: tends to sit on the surface and improve feel/comfort.
  • Lower weight / hydrolyzed forms: often feel lighter, sometimes more “active” on reactive skin.
  • Crosspolymer forms: can create longer-lasting surface hydration and a smoother finish.

In reality, the supporting ingredients (glycerin, panthenol, betaine, urea) and your top-layer moisturiser usually matter more than chasing a specific molecular weight claim.

Template A: “Hydration-first” (dehydrated/tight skin)

  1. Cleanser
  2. HA serum on damp skin (thin layer)
  3. Moisturiser (medium-rich)
  4. AM: SPF

Template B: “Actives + hydration” (retinoids/acids)

  1. Cleanser
  2. Actives (vitamin C AM / retinoid PM)
  3. HA serum (optional, very thin)
  4. Moisturiser (barrier-supporting)
  5. AM: SPF
Anti-pilling rule: keep each layer thin, and wait 30–60 seconds between layers.

Formula red flags (what to avoid)

  • Overly thick gels that dry down like glue (common cause of tightness).
  • Strong fragrance if you’re sensitive or barrier-compromised.
  • Multiple HA products stacked (usually redundant and increases tackiness).
  • No “seal” step (if you don’t use moisturiser, the routine often fails).

Advanced HA FAQ

Is “multi-molecular weight” always better?

Not always. It can help, but formula design and your moisturiser matter more. A simple HA serum + good cream often beats a complex serum used incorrectly.

Should I use HA before or after actives?

Either can work. If your active is irritating, apply it first, then HA + moisturiser to restore comfort. If your active pills, keep HA minimal or skip it and focus on moisturiser.

What’s the best quick fix for tightness?

Apply HA on damp skin and switch to a slightly richer moisturiser on top. In very dry air, consider a humidifier.
